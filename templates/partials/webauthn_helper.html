{# WebAuthn / passkey UX helper. This runs after Kratos's served `webauthn.js`. Two responsibilities: 1. **Platform-credential detection.** Some browsers (LibreWolf/Firefox on Linux without TPM, older Android WebViews, etc.) advertise WebAuthn support but lack any platform authenticator. Clicking a passkey button there produces a silent DOMException. We detect this at page load and disable any passkey trigger with an inline explanation, rather than letting users hit a broken button. 2. **Runtime error surfacing.** Kratos's reference webauthn.js does `.catch(err => console.error(err))` — failures are silent from the user's POV. We override `console.error` (lightly) to forward DOMException-shaped errors to a visible banner above the form. The override preserves the original behaviour (logs still appear in devtools); it just adds a user-visible signal. No-op when no passkey/webauthn buttons are present on the page. #}
